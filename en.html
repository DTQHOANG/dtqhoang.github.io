03 — Working to brief & taking feedback
Learning from feedback.

This is a project I did for Nhi Lê's team (not part of my own channel) — a real example of how I work with clients: taking feedback on board and shifting my mindset, from simply following the brief to staying true to the goal.

● Before · not usable
Draft
Birthday wishes — first cut
Off target · leaned toward "showing off"
The goal was to wish someone a happy birthday, but the video felt more like a showreel than a heartfelt message — so it wasn't usable.
My mindset back thenI just made exactly what the brief said, without asking what this video was actually meant to achieve.
● After · on target
Final cut
A surprise gift for Nhi
On target · a warm birthday message
After taking the feedback, I reworked it to hit the goal: a genuine birthday wish instead of a showreel.
My mindset nowFirst ask what the video's goal is, think about the big picture (the overall message), then move to the small picture (shots, text, editing pace).
Watch on Nhi Lê's YouTube channel ↗
What I took away

A great editor doesn't just follow the brief — they understand the real goal behind it. I learn fast, listen to feedback, and adjust my thinking so every video serves exactly what the client needs.
